Mesh block is a smallest geographical area that is defined by the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS), and they form the larger regions of the Australian Statistical Geography Standard (ASGS). They are mainly used for identifying land usage such as residential, commercial, primary production and parks, etc. Each one of them are very small and therefore they can be combined together to accurately approximate a larger range of other regions.

Statistical Area Level 1 (SA1) are one of the geographical areas that are made from mesh blocks. Currently there are 57,523 spatial SA1 regions covering Australia without gaps or overlaps.
